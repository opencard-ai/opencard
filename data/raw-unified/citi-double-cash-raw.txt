We estimate that ThankYou Points (TYP) are worth about 1.6 cents/point, see below for a brief introduction. Even if you don&#8217;t want to spend time to study the redemption of points and miles, you can choose to redeem TYP at a fixed ratio 1.0 cent/point towards cash.</li><li>No annual fee.</li></ol><h2><span id="Disadvantages">Disadvantages</span></h2><ol><li>It has foreign transaction fee, so it's not a good choice outside the US.</li></ol><h2><span id="Introduction_to_TYP">Introduction to TYP</span></h2><ul><li>You can earn TYP with <a href="https://www.uscreditcardguide.com/citi-strata-elite-credit-card/" target="_blank">Citi Strata Elite</a>, <a href="https://www.uscreditcardguide.com/citi-thankyou-premier-credit-card/" target="_blank">Citi Strata Premier</a>, <a href="https://www.uscreditcardguide.com/citi-strata-credit-card/" target="_blank">Citi Strata</a>, <a href="https://www.uscreditcardguide.com/citi-custom-cash-credit-card/" target="_blank">Citi Custom Cash</a>, <a href="https://www.uscreditcardguide.com/citi-double-cash-credit-card/" target="_blank">Citi Double Cash</a>, etc.</li><li>You can add all these cards into the same thankyou.com account, and points that are about to expire will be redeemed first in the order in which they expire automatically.</li><li>In most cases, TYP never expire. But closing account, product change, or receiving points from other people may cause TYP on that account to expire.</li><li>If you have <a href="https://www.uscreditcardguide.com/citi-thankyou-premier-credit-card/" target="_blank">Citi Strata Premier</a> or <a href="https://www.uscreditcardguide.com/citi-strata-elite-credit-card/" target="_blank">Citi Strata Elite</a> or <a href="https://www.uscreditcardguide.com/citi-prestige-credit-card/" target="_blank">Citi Prestige (Discontinued)</a>, TYP can be transferred to some airline miles. The best way to use TYP is to 1:1 transfer them to American Airlines miles (Oneworld) or EVA Air (BR) miles (Star Alliance). Other good options are: Cathay Pacific (CX) miles (Oneworld), Avianca (AV) miles (Star Alliance), Singapore Airlines (SQ) miles (Star Alliance), Flying Blue miles  (SkyTeam), Virgin Atlantic (VS) miles (Non-alliance), etc. If you use TYP in this way, the value is about 1.6 cents/point.</li><li>You can redeem your TYP at a fixed rate 1 cents/point towards cash.</li><li>If you have <a href="https://www.uscreditcardguide.com/citi-prestige-credit-card/" target="_blank">Citi Prestige (Discontinued)</a>, you can redeem your TYP at a fixed rate 1.25 cents/point towards air tickets on <a href="https://www.thankyou.com" target="_blank">thankyou.com</a>. This is a common way to use TYP. Points+cash is available when redeeming for air tickets, so no need to worry if you do not have enough TYP.</li><li><s>If you have <a href="https://www.uscreditcardguide.com/citi-rewards-plus-credit-card/" target="_blank">Citi Rewards+ (Discontinued)</a>, you can get 10% back when redeeming TYP, up to 100k TYP per year. This feature further boosts the value of TYP.</s> [Expired]</li><li>In summary, we estimate that TYP are worth about 1.6 cents/point.</li><li>For more information about TYP, see <a href="https://www.uscreditcardguide.com/maximize-the-credit-card-points-values/" target="_blank">Maximize the Credit Card Points Values</a> (overview), and <a href="https://www.uscreditcardguide.com/introduction-to-citi-thankyou-point1-how-to-earn/" target="_blank">Introduction to TYP: How to Earn</a> & <a href="https://www.uscreditcardguide.com/introduction-to-citi-thankyou-points-ii-how-to-use/" target="_blank">Introduction to TYP: How to Use</a> (very detailed).</li></ul><h2><span id="Recommended_Application_Time">Recommended Application Time</span></h2><ol><li>[8/65 Rule] You can apply for at most 1 Citi cards every 8 days, and at most 2 Citi cards every 65 days, no matter approved or not.</li><li>Citi values the number of recent hard pulls a lot, we recommend you apply when there&#8217;s less than 6 hard pulls in the past 6 months.</li><li>We recommend you apply for this card after you have a credit history of at least one year.</li></ol><h2><span id="Summary">Summary</span></h2><p>This card was already a no-brainer for most people when it offered only 2% cashback. Now that you have the option to convert cashback to ThankYou Points (TYP), the DC has become the most valuable no-annual fee card on the market in my opinion. You should use this card for all transactions that don&#8217;t have 2%+ reward rate. There&#8217;s no standard sign-up bonus on this card, so instead of applying for this card directly, we recommend you the DC by downgrading other Citi cards once you receive the signup bonus. According to Citi&#8217;s policy, all the accounts older than 12 months can be downgraded to this card.
